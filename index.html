Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Multiple projects shared above involved rhetoric, technology, and ethics in the acts of making. In particular, Design Fuego introduced me to site development. In this process, I learned how to include those who are visually impared by including captions for my images. Additionally, the UX Design Project presented rhetoric as a challenge while writing participant questions. To elaborate, I learned to write neutral, unbiased questions to ensure unbiased responses.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

I utilized the prinicples of design in each project I completed. However, the one that used them most was the Good Design project. In this project, we selected an example of good design, and then used Adobe Indesign to create a poster explaining its "good design" elements. This made me think outside of the box and analyze my design more deeply overall.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

After each of our projects, we worked in groups to get feedback from classmates about our projects. This created a collaborative environment where we could improve our projects. This was especially useful in the UX Design Study, where we discussed our interview scripts. This ensured there were multiple perspectives that could be used to create the best product possible.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Each project was revised multiple times using media like Adobe Indesign, Canva, and Google Slides. Outside revising my work during production, I often went back and changed aspects after receiving peer reviews. This taught me how to take user feedback and employ it to create the best product possible. This was especially important in the UX Design Study, where participants gave feedback about each Logo. From there, I took their perspectives and used them to create an ideal final design.

5. Learn to present your work.

After each project, we shared our work within small groups. For the UX Design Project, we even presented our work using Google Slides. This taught me confidence when presenting my work on platforms I am not yet comfortable using. For the Cartoon Self Portrait, my work was shown to the class for demonstration purposes. While this was nerve wracking, I felt proud of my work in the end.
